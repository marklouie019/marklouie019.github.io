Nara

Japan's first permanent capital

Nara, located just outside of Kyoto, was Japan’s first permanent capital and is home to some of the country’s oldest and most significant historical treasures. Among its highlights is Todai-ji, a massive temple housing the world’s largest bronze Buddha statue, and Nara Park, where over 1,000 friendly, free-roaming deer — considered sacred messengers of the gods — greet visitors. These tame deer have become one of Nara's most famous attractions.

Nara’s wealth of UNESCO World Heritage sites, including Kasuga Taisha Shrine and Kofuku-ji Temple, offer a window into Japan’s early history and religious practices. With its ancient landmarks and serene natural beauty, Nara provides a peaceful retreat and a deeper understanding of Japan's cultural foundations.
