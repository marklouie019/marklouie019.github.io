Mt.Fuji

Japan's most iconic landmark

Mount Fuji, Japanâ€™s tallest and most iconic peak, stands at 3,776 meters and is revered not just for its beauty, but also for its cultural and spiritual significance. This majestic volcano, often capped with snow, has been a source of inspiration for artists, poets, and pilgrims for centuries. Its nearly symmetrical shape makes it a symbol of Japan's natural beauty, and it holds an important place in Japanese mythology and religious practices, with many considering it sacred.

Every year, thousands of climbers ascend Mount Fuji during the summer months to witness breathtaking sunrise views from its summit, an experience known as "Goraiko." Whether admired from afar or experienced up close, Mount Fuji continues to be a symbol of Japan's enduring connection to nature and tradition, attracting both locals and visitors alike to bask in its grandeur.
